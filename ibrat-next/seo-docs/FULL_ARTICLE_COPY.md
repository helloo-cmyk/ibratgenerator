### Key Characteristics of Brat Typography

The brat font style is defined by six consistent design principles.

#### Bold weight

Heavy strokes at 700+ weight. The text feels dense and grounded, not thin or decorative. This is what separates brat typography from standard body text.

#### Clean sans-serif forms

No serifs, no swashes, no ornamentation. The letterforms follow geometric sans-serif construction with even stroke widths and open counters.

#### Tight letter spacing

Characters sit close together with deliberate, compact gaps. Tight letter spacing gives the text a solid, connected appearance that reads as a single visual block.

#### High contrast

Dark text on bright backgrounds or light text on deep black. The brat typography aesthetic depends on strong separation between foreground and background.

#### Minimal ornamentation

The text is the design. No gradients, no shadows, no outlines. Just type on a flat color field.

#### Centered or deliberate layouts

Text is placed with intention. Centered alignment is most common, but offset compositions work when the asymmetry is clearly purposeful.

### What Font Is Used In The Brat Album Style?

The Brat album style does not use a single named commercial font. It draws from a lineage of geometric sans-serif typefaces that prioritize clarity, weight, and minimal decoration. Designers and fans often compare it to Helvetica Neue Bold, Avenir Heavy, or Gotham, all of which share clean geometric forms and strong x-heights. The key is not letter-by-letter matching but the overall impression: heavy weight, tight letter spacing, and a sense of confidence rooted in Swiss design typography.
